About

I specialize in analytics and consulting, building the automated pipelines and decision-support tools that help healthcare leaders make strategic choices. Most of my work involves engaging with stakeholders, taking messy, fragmented institutional data, and transforming it into clean, reliable systems that clinical and operational teams can trust.

My background started in biomedical research, specifically molecular genetics and biochemistry, where I learned to think carefully about complex, uncertain problems. I eventually wanted work where that analytical thinking would have immediate operational impact, so I moved into business analytics, starting in healthcare supply chain. There I designed data solutions that helped identify over $2 million in cost reductions through standardization. Today I apply that same focus to healthcare operations, working on problems like census validation, sterile processing workflows, and standardizing operational reporting across multiple hospital entities.

Along the way I realized I wanted to round out the software engineering side of my background, so I completed an MS in Computer Science at Georgia Tech with a specialization in Human-Computer Interaction. The HCI focus was deliberate: a technically sound system is only useful if people can actually work with it. Going forward, I want to bring that CS foundation together with my analytics background to build tools that do not just describe what happened, but help teams make better decisions as things unfold.
